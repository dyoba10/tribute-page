About

Yoasobi is a Japanese music duo composed of Vocaloid (voice synthesizer software) producer Ayase and singer-songwriter Ikura (Lilas Ikuta). The group has released songs based on short stories posted on Monogatary, a website operated by Sony Music Entertainment Japan, later also from various novels and book tie-ups.

History


2019–2020: Formation and rising up with "Yoru ni Kakeru"

Both members of Yoasobi had active music careers prior to forming the group. Ayase gained a following on the video hosting site Niconico since his first upload on December 24, 2018, a song titled "Sentensei Assault Girl" (先天性アサルトガール, Sentensei Asaruto Gāru). On November 23, 2019, he released an extended play, Yūrei Tōkyō (幽霊東京), which reached third on iTunes' album chart.Lilas Ikuta (幾田りら, Ikuta Rira), performing under the name Ikura, had already released two extended plays: Rerise in 2017 and Jukebox in 2019.


The first song released by the duo was "Yoru ni Kakeru", based on Mayo Hoshino's short story Thanatos no Yūwaku hosted on Monogatary. The music video was released first on November 16, 2019, where it broke 10 million views in five months on YouTube as well as being popular on TikTok. It was subsequently released as a single on December 15, 2019, where it topped the popularity charts on both Spotify and Line Music.[10] More than five months after its release, the single would top the Billboard Japan Hot 100 on June 1, 2020. The song also top the year-end chart of Billboard Japan Hot 100, become the first song not released on CD to top the year-end chart.


Following the success of their first release, the duo released their second single, "Ano Yume o Nazotte" on January 18, 2020. The song is based on Sōta Ishiki's Yume no Shizuku to Hoshi no Hana. On April 20, the duo released the teaser for their third single, "Halzion", which is based on Shunki Hashizume's Soredemo, Happy End. "Halzion" marked the first time that Yoasobi collaborated with a professional novelist, whereas the first two were amateur writers. The single was released as a promotional project by Suntory to advertise their new energy drink named Zone. The single was officially released on May 11.


In the second half of the year, Yoasobi released their fourth single "Tabun" on July 20. The song is based on the short story of the same name by Shinano. On September 1, the duo released their fifth single, "Gunjō". The song was collaborated together with the manga, Blue Period for Bourbon's Alfort Mini Chocolate advertisement, and based on Ao o Mikata ni., Alfort's television advertisement story text.[16] On December 18, the duo released their sixth single "Haruka", based on Osamu Suzuki's novel, Tsuki Ōji. On December 31, 2020, they debuted in 71st NHK Kōhaku Uta Gassen and performed "Yoru ni Kakeru", which is also their first live performance.



2021–present: The Book

On January 6, 2021, Yoasobi released their first extended play and first physical release The Book, included the 6 previous tracks and 3 new tracks, "Encore" as a promotional track, "Epilogue" as an intro, and "Prologue" as am outro. The duo also released their seventh single, "Kaibutsu", in the same day. The song has been appointed as the opening theme song for the second season of the anime television series Beastars. The anime's ending theme song and the duo's eighth single "Yasashii Suisei", was released on January 20, 2021. The both was released as a first double A-side single in physical format on March 24, 2021.
